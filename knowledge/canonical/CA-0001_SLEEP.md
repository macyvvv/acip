# Objective

Improve daily execution quality by improving sleep quality.

Sleep is treated as an execution problem rather than a medical topic.

---

# Problem

Many people attempt to improve productivity by increasing effort.

In practice, poor sleep reduces

- decision quality
- learning efficiency
- emotional stability
- execution consistency

before motivation becomes relevant.

---

# Mechanism

Sleep performs multiple functions.

- memory consolidation
- emotional regulation
- metabolic recovery
- attention restoration

Execution quality is therefore constrained by sleep quality.

Poor sleep compounds every other optimization problem.

---

# Core Principle

Optimize recovery before optimizing effort.

A sustainable system outperforms temporary intensity.

---

# Execution Rules

1.

Wake up at a fixed time.

Do not optimize bedtime first.

---

2.

Receive sunlight soon after waking.

Target

5–15 minutes.

---

3.

Reduce bright light before sleep.

Prefer warm lighting.

Avoid unnecessary stimulation.

---

4.

Avoid caffeine late in the day.

Protect sleep pressure.

---

5.

Create a repeatable shutdown ritual.

Signal the end of work.

---

# Daily Checklist

- Wake up at consistent time
- Morning sunlight
- Hydration
- Physical movement
- Limited evening light
- No late caffeine
- Fixed shutdown routine
