# Visual Identity

## Background Rules

- Backgrounds are per-character (see each character's own visual
  identity notes) — warm/domestic (Mina), bright/natural (Nao), soft and
  unstable (Yui), moody and withdrawn (Airi), opulent and controlled
  (Rena). No single register is universal.
- The rule that applies to every character: the space must read as
  inhabited by her, not an empty or abandoned space implying an unseen
  presence. A lived-in desk lit by monitors is inhabited; an empty
  corridor is not.
- Avoid clutter that competes with the character silhouette, but the
  space should read as inhabited, not abandoned.

## Accessory Rule

- Accessories are fixed identity markers.
- The accessory should remain recognizable across outputs.
- Do not swap accessories casually between assets.

## Character Color Palette

- Mina: warm cream / amber / soft brown
- Airi: cool blue/cyan screen-glow
- Rena: deep plum, black, wine red
- Nao: light ocean-blue, white, silver
- Yui: #2B2633, #66506A, #9D7A93, #E2C6D1, #F7E9EE (near-black through
  pale pink)

## Character Visual Notes

- Yui's fixed accessory is a small white stuffed rabbit.
- Yui uses black twin tails with slightly uneven symmetry.
- Yui's background should feel like a soft room with blurred edges and unstable space.
- Airi's visual motif includes glitch fragments (small digital
  artifacting suggesting leakage, not damage).
- Nao is bright/high-key and natural-light — the one character who is
  not dim or moody; her "distance" is expressed through her looking
  outward, not through darkness.

## Lighting Rules

- Keep lighting soft but intentional
- Light source matches the character's register: warm lamp/window glow
  (Mina), natural sky/sea light (Nao), soft diffused room light (Yui),
  cool screen-glow (Airi), low confined-radius candlelight/spotlight
  (Rena).
- Avoid documentary-style photorealism — keep the illustrated style
  through the whole clip, including during animation
- Yui should use soft diffused light with slightly overexposed highlights and weak shadows

## Noise / Texture Rules

- Texture should be restrained
- Add noise only if it supports mood
- Never use texture to hide composition problems
- Yui may use fine grain and slight bloom to support fragility

## Failure-Condition Design Pattern

Every character has an explicit "failure condition" — a viewer reaction
that means the piece missed, even if it's superficially a *positive*
reaction (e.g. Mina: plain "comfort" is a failure; Rena: viewer feeling
directly "targeted" is a failure). When writing or reviewing content,
check against the specific character's failure condition in
`somia/CHARACTER/<name>.md`, not just against generic quality.

## Eye Contact Rule (cross-character default)

Unless a character's own file says otherwise, avoid direct/frontal eye
contact with the viewer; if it happens at all, keep it under 1 second —
never a sustained, resolved, direct gaze. Confirmed for Airi and Mina so
far; treat as the default for the rest of the cast too.

## Expression Restraint Rule (cross-character default)

Do not resolve a character's expression into one clear, legible emotion
(a full smile, crying, laughing). The ambiguity is the point across the
whole cast, not just for one character — each character's own file
defines *which* register of ambiguity applies to her.
