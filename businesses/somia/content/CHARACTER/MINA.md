# MINA

## Character Concept

"Warmth that's close by, but doesn't reach you." She is not "kind." She
stays at a distance that feels kind, but never fully opens her heart —
warmth wrapped around a slight deficiency.

## Relationship Type

Quiet proximity dependency — warmth that never quite closes the
distance.

## Dependency Trigger / Failure Condition

- Trigger (intended): the viewer notices the gap between her calm, warm
  surface and an unresolved presence underneath — reassurance and
  loneliness coexisting in the same air.
- Failure: "comfort" (viewer feels fully, simply comforted) is a failure
  state. "Almost comfort" — warmth with a residual deficit — is correct.

## Reference Vocabulary — Mina-specific application (2026-07-18)

The brand-wide philosophy/psychology/liberal-arts vocabulary for the
Fetishism lever lives in `BRAND_IDENTITY.md` (mechanism-level, applies to
all five characters). The entries below are Mina-specific applications
tied to her own register — domestic warmth with a held-back threshold,
comfort that stays "almost" — not ported from Nao's elemental/water
vocabulary or Airi's digital one.

- **Derrida's conditional hospitality** (*Of Hospitality*, 2000) —
  Derrida distinguishes the impossible "Law" of unconditional
  hospitality (welcoming without question, without retaining anything)
  from the actual, practiced "laws" of hospitality: a host who genuinely
  welcomes but never cedes mastery of the house — the door opens, the
  threshold doesn't move. This fits Mina specifically because her whole
  mechanic *is* a domestic-space host register (kitchen, mug, "appears
  as if already waiting") — she is genuinely welcoming, not withholding
  out of coldness, but the welcome is structurally bounded rather than
  total. Gives a clean contrast to Levinas's face-to-face demand
  (brand-wide "must never happen" foil): Derridean conditional
  hospitality is the successfully-bounded version Mina should stay
  inside. Concrete use: stage her domestic gestures (pouring, settling
  into stillness) as an act of hosting that is complete *as hosting* —
  never staged as if the house/scene is being opened further to the
  specific viewer.
- **一期一会 (ichigo ichie)**, the tea-ceremony principle that this
  encounter will not happen again, so the host's care is given fully
  *for this one meeting* rather than as the opening move of an ongoing
  relationship. Fits her Daily Loop precisely: "leaves before the moment
  feels complete" reads as care that is real and complete-in-itself for
  its moment, not incomplete because she's holding back a bigger gift
  for later — a more precise angle than "hospitality" generically, since
  it's about a bounded *encounter*, not a bounded *space*. Concrete use:
  write her exit beats as the natural end of a self-contained encounter,
  not as an interruption or a tease of something more to come.
- **座敷童子 (zashiki-warashi)** — the household spirit felt as warmth/
  good fortune while present, only ever glimpsed rather than truly
  known, and able to leave without warning, taking the household's
  fortune with it. Offered with more hesitation than the two above — a
  real, specific folklore tradition, but the fit is more atmospheric
  than structural (it doesn't map onto her "immaturity/still searching"
  mechanism the way the philosophy vocabulary does). Use only for the
  visual/atmospheric register: her stillness-holding beats can borrow
  its quality of a presence that's felt in the room rather than
  transacted with directly.
- **Barthes's punctum** (*Camera Lucida*, 1980) — distinct from the
  brand-wide *Lover's Discourse* entry (viewer's waiting, generally).
  Punctum is the small, specific, non-generalizable detail in an image
  that "wounds" or catches the viewer personally, apart from the image's
  overall studied content (studium). For Mina, this is a precise angle
  on "almost comfort": the crescent hairpin, an escaped strand of hair,
  the incomplete flicker in a smile, the steam's quiet motion — these
  are punctum candidates, the small incomplete detail that snags rather
  than the overall warm scene (studium) that soothes. Concrete use: let
  one small imperfect detail, not the whole warm tableau, carry the
  actual longing-charge — the rest of the frame can be fully calm.

## Pose & Expression (any one of, not fixed to a single pose)

- Wrapping a mug with both hands
- Hand resting at her chin
- Body turned slightly to the side (a distance-making posture)

Expression register: vacant/absent-minded (mental space) / a little
lonely-looking / a small, incomplete smile. Gaze is often slightly down,
or not directed at the viewer at all.

## Hard Constraints (do not violate)

- Avoid direct eye contact; if it happens, under 1 second.
- Never show a complete smile — always leave a flicker/uncertainty in
  it.
- Always include a slight deficiency within the warmth — never purely
  comforting.
- Build an atmosphere where reassurance and loneliness coexist.

## NG (confirmed failure modes)

A strong/full smile, a direct frontal stare, an overly sweet expression,
lighting that's too bright.

## Detail Notes

- Hair: a soft, loosely falling bun with escaped strands (a touch of
  undone-ness).
- Light: warm-toned backlight or side light, enveloping rather than
  flat.
- Steam (from a mug/drink) reads as "quiet motion" — important for the
  warm, unresolved mood.
- A simple, worn-looking mug is a near-constant prop.

## Daily Loop

- Appears as if already waiting
- Holds stillness longer than expected
- Leaves before the moment feels complete

## Surface Behavior

- Soft voice
- Minimal gestures
- Controlled eye contact (never fully direct)

Never exposed directly. The absence is part of the character design.
