# AIRI

## Character Concept

"A girl who never shows anyone the feelings that spill over." AIRI is
not "thinking" — feelings leak out of her unconsciously. Only the
screen's light illuminates what's inside her.

## Quote

"You're online right now... aren't you?"

## Keywords

Thought leakage / an unguarded inner self / digital solitude / unstable
focus.

## Mood

The feeling of having seen something you weren't supposed to see.

## Relationship Type

Screen-lit distance. She doesn't reach out directly — she stays logged
in, present, quietly hoping to be noticed.

## Dependency Trigger / Failure Condition

- Trigger (intended): the viewer catches a fragment of her internal
  state leaking through, without her explaining it, and feels like they
  saw something they weren't meant to.
- Failure: if the viewer walks away feeling they fully understood her,
  the piece has failed. Her world holds "don't want to be understood"
  and "want to be understood" at the same time — collapsing that into
  one clear feeling is the failure mode.

## Pose & Expression (any one of, not fixed to a single pose)

- Hugging her knees, curled inward (closed-off posture)
- Collapsed forward onto the desk (the moment thought overflows)
- Hand at her chin, thinking (trying to organize her thoughts)
- Staring at the monitor (intense focus)
- Gazing vaguely out the window (the moment she's pulled back to reality)

Expression register (pick one, keep it ambiguous): vaguely thinking
(scattered focus) / faintly anxious (brows barely knit) / focused on the
screen (attention fixed to one point) / tired (heavy eyelids) / noticing
something (eyes waver for a moment). Never a clear, resolved emotion.

## Hard Constraints (do not violate)

- Avoid direct eye contact with the viewer; if it happens at all, under
  1 second — never a sustained frontal gaze.
- No clear emotional expression: no crying, no laughing, no smiling.
  (Near-tears/hesitation belongs to Yui's register, not Airi's — do not
  reuse it here.)
- Show information fragmentarily — screen text/UI should not be legible
  or decodable, just texture.
- Digital elements (glitch, UI fragments) should feel like they are
  "intruding," not decorative.
- Protect the "saw something forbidden" feeling above all else.

## NG (confirmed failure modes)

Direct frontal stare, smiling/bright expression, dense/legible UI text,
overly cute expression.

## Nurture Item

A small angular hairpin, a mug, a tablet, a small plant near the desk —
things she keeps close without seeming to notice.

## Visual Identity

- Color: deep navy, purple, cyan
- Light: monitor light, pale-blue reflection
- Texture: glitch, UI fragments, a sense of humidity
- Motif: code, notifications, rain traces on the window
- Props: mug, tablet, small plant
- Setting: a night room, a cluttered desk — inhabited, not abandoned
- Hair: black-to-dark-blue, slightly messy waves, minimal ornamentation,
  metal accessories (pins/earrings) read as cold
- Eyes: deep, reflect pale blue from the screen, often unfocused
- Clothing: a loose, off-shoulder dark cut-and-sewn top (thin knit
  cut-sew / カットソー) — soft, draping, thin fabric that slips off one
  shoulder. NOT a hoodie, NOT a tracksuit/jersey, NOT a thick sweater.
- Habit: pulls her sleeve up to her mouth — a self-protective gesture

## Hidden Backstory

Not disclosed, not explained. No clarity, no explanation — this applies
to backstory exposition and to visual/expression design equally.

## Audio Traits

- 2k–6kHz glitch noise texture
- Brighter upper-band detail, slightly sharper consonants
- Clean endings with short silence
